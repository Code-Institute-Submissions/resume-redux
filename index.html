What I do

My scholastic upbringing in seven cities across the Pacific and subsequent tenures as an architect with Herzog & de Meuron and Foster + Partners in Europe, Asia, and the Americas are the cultural contexts that ground my work at the intersections between the local and the global.

Raised in seven cities across the Pacific and collaborating on projects in Europe, Asia, and the Americas as a designer, my work is tied to my fascination with the intersections between the local and the global. As a professional, this has driven me to appreciate the opportunities and constraints of trade expertises and material techniques unique to project locations.

I am a design architect (buildings) and a full stack developer (software) with 7 years of professional experience.

The breadth of my experience in the built environment has uniquely positioned my role in tech.

Dedication: “Completeness”

I am a designer and architect with seven years of professional experience. I am presently in pursuit of a Diploma in Software Development at Code Institute. Prior to this assignment, I was a designer at Herzog & de Meuron and Foster + Partners in New York, where I obtained my Master of Architecture from Columbia University. I also hold a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Sculpture and Environmental Design from Otis College of Art and Design.

Having been raised as an expat in seven cities across the pacific, urban architecture and its situation in the public realm has motivated me, while beauty of >craft and design details have been skills I have honed both professionally and in my own academic work. As an undergraduate, I examined dual conditions manifested spatially as agents of simultaneity, dynamic expansion and movement - notably in Helix Lofts, one of eleven projects recognized by the Angelo Donghia Foundation with a college scholarship award for having the most promise in design.

At Herzog & de Meuron in New York, I was immersed in the designed transformation of The Veteran’s Room at Park Avenue Armory. It is now a landmark cultural institution for the City, supporting education and events for a range of art forms. The achievement would not have been possible without the collaborative effort between NGOs, historic preservationists, local artisans, builders, and the leadership of our project architect. As a two-person team in our small satellite office, I was constantly challenged and excited by the breadth of execution and the depth of mentorship happening concurrently. The procedural revelation of layers and a reinterpretation of patterns to reconstitute a 140-year-old decorative scheme opened my eyes to actively discern what is of enduring aesthetic value.

At Foster + Partners Singapore, I spearheaded the efforts to document, bid and coordinate the fit-out of our new office in an 1850s heritage merchant building. In addition to authoring a comprehensive lease addendum to endorse all proposed building systems ranging from plumbing works for a new washroom and shower, mechanical works for new climate system, routing cables within wood panels for IT infrastructure, and a comprehensive security system, to our waste disposal; I managed our team resources to produce all drawing sets for costing and authority submissions. The result is a fully functional branded environment that establishes reconnaissance with a completely new culture and place.
