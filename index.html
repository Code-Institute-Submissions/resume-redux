The breadth of my experience in the built environment has uniquely positioned my role in tech.

Dedication: “Completeness”

At Herzog & de Meuron in New York, I was immersed in the designed transformation of The Veteran’s Room at Park Avenue Armory. It is now a landmark cultural institution for the City, supporting education and events for a range of art forms. The achievement would not have been possible without the collaborative effort between NGOs, historic preservationists, local artisans, builders, and the leadership of our project architect. As a two-person team in our small satellite office, I was constantly challenged and excited by the breadth of execution and the depth of mentorship happening concurrently. The procedural revelation of layers and a reinterpretation of patterns to reconstitute a 140-year-old decorative scheme opened my eyes to actively discern what is of enduring aesthetic value.

At Foster + Partners Singapore, I spearheaded the efforts to document, bid and coordinate the fit-out of our new office in an 1850s heritage merchant building. In addition to authoring a comprehensive lease addendum to endorse all proposed building systems ranging from plumbing works for a new washroom and shower, mechanical works for new climate system, routing cables within wood panels for IT infrastructure, and a comprehensive security system, to our waste disposal; I managed our team resources to produce all drawing sets for costing and authority submissions. The result is a fully functional branded environment that establishes reconnaissance with a completely new culture and place.

I am able to approach problems from an angle that, at best and most applicable, is evident in practice and current in an evolving experience industry and/or, at the very least, an angle that is different and unexplored.

While thorough and iterative testing of big ideas of which results are measured, organized and delivered without delay have been features I ascribe directly to the completeness I dedicate to every task; I remain insatiable of the software that delivered the task that measured the results that organized the iterations.

Most Lauded for: Documentation Style

In my first weeks as a trainee at Herzog & de Meuron in Basel, I identified our critical need for a comprehensive digital model of our Skolkovo Institute proposal in Moscow and worked closely with 30 designers to translate their two-dimensional drawings into a detailed digital model that I developed from scratch. Not only did this model allow us to produce 20+ renderings of the project on an accelerated 3-week delivery, it also gave me an opportunity to quickly build working relationships and establish myself as a dependable team member. As a result of my contributions in Basel, I was hired as an architectural assistant in Herzog & de Meuron’s New York office after resuming my graduate studies at Columbia. As an integral member of this 8-person satellite team, I gained new insight into construction administration while working with clients to integrate extensive design changes into a large-scale residential project and overseeing the fabrication of interior fittings for the adaptive reuse of the Park Avenue Armory.

As a trained architect with 7 years of professional experience, in design coordination, I am particularly diligent about quality documentation. I write code that is readable, well-formatted, well-tested, well-documented. As a developer the pragmatism to which I approach planning and coordinate is displayed in my repository and repository commit history.

My approach to documentation/representation that is as conscientious as it is innovative.

Most Lauded for: Documentation Style

My experiences working between the local and the global have propelled a conceptual fascination with duality and simultaneity across many scales in my own academic work. My final-semester project, Double Jointed, imagined a world obsessed with mechanical hinges and recombinant tectonics. The undergraduate design-build project I led, Landscape Extrusion, explored such conjoined conditions through the realistic material constraints of welded steel. My work is driven by an ambition to challenge the default settings of urban form and enabled by my commitment to thorough testing and representation of my ideas. I take pride in my ability to communicate through many different media, a skill strengthened by my transnational upbringing, and in my proficiency at rapidly developing digital and physical models to visualize design iterations. In addition to being recruited as a studio mentor at Columbia, my design work has been recognized with several scholarship awards from the Angelo Donghia Foundation and honorable mentions from the Los Angeles chapter of the AIA and the LABC Architectural Awards.
